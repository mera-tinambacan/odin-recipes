JOLLIBEE STYLE BURGER STEAK: THE ULTIMATE COMFORT FOOD THAT HITS THE SPOT

Jollibee's Burger Steak captures hearts everywhere, and now you can bring that magic to your own kitchen with this Jollibee Style Burger Steak Recipe! Imagine sinking your teeth into juicy, flavorful beef patties drenched in a rich, savory mushroom gravy. Pair it with steaming white rice, and you’ve got a comforting, filling meal that feels just like your favorite fast-food treat—but homemade.

Ingredients:

Patty:
  • 300 g Lean Beef & 100g Pork or 400 g Ground Beef
  • 5 tbsp. Bread Crumbs
  • 3 tbsp. Evap or Fresh Milk
  • ½ tsp. Salt & Pepper to taste
  • 2 tsp. Knorr Liquid Seasoning
  • 3 tbsp. Onion, minced
  • 1 med. Egg
  • 4 tbsp. Oil for frying
Gravy:
  • 2 tbsp. Butter or Margarine
  • ½ cup Mushroom slices
  • 2 tbsp. Flour
  • 1¼ cups Hot Water
  • ½ cube Knorr Beef Cube
  • ¼ tsp. Black Pepper
  • 1 tsp. Soy Sauce

Procedures:

Instructions

Burger Patty

Prepare the Mixture:
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine breadcrumbs and milk.
  2. Let the mixture sit for 2-3 minutes until the breadcrumbs soften.
  3. Add ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork for extra flavor).
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and a splash of liquid seasoning for depth.
  5. Toss in finely minced onion and crack in an egg. Mix everything thoroughly until well combined.
Shape the Patties:
  1. Divide the meat mixture into 6 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball, then flatten it gently with your hands to form a patty.
  2. Place each patty on a sheet of wax paper to prevent sticking.
  3. Arrange them in a container and freeze for at least 30 minutes to firm up. (Thaw for 10-15 minutes before cooking if frozen.)
Cook the Patties:
  1. Heat a thin layer of oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefolly add the patties.
  2. Fry for 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown and cooked through.
  3. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even cooking.
  4. Remove the patties and set them aside on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Mushroom Gravy

Sauté the Mushrooms:
  1. In the same pan, melt butter over low heat.
  2. Add sliced mushrooms and sauté for 1-2 minutes until they soften and release their aroma.
Make the Roux:
  1. Sprinkle flour over the mushrooms and stir continuously for 2 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste.
Create the Gravy:
  1. Gradually pour in hot water while stirring constantly to prevent lumps.
  2. Add a beef cube, a dash of soy sauce, and a pinch of black pepper for seasoning.
  3. Let the gravy simmer for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens to your desired consistency.
Combine and Serve:
  1. Place the cooked burger patties on a plate, pour the hot mushroom gravy over them, and serve with steamed rice.
